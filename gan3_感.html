字源zìyuán Etymology & Structure
字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight

感 gǎn = 咸 xián (all; universal; reaching everywhere) + 心 xīn (heart). The oracle-bone form of 咸 shows a battle-axe striking a mouth — a sound that reaches all ears. Placed above 心, it becomes: that which the heart universally resonates with. This is why 感 covers such vast emotional terrain: sensation, gratitude, inspiration — all are versions of the heart being struck and vibrating. In Japanese, the same character is read kan (感じる kanjiru "to feel," 感謝 kansha "gratitude") — the semantic field maps perfectly, giving learners of both languages a powerful cross-linguistic anchor.

感知gǎnzhī Core Perception Verbs
感到gǎn dàoto feel; to register a state
V 动词 dòngcí
感 gǎn (feel) + 到 dào (arrive at · result marker). The 到 signals the feeling has landed — a completed, registered state. The most neutral, all-purpose feeling verb. Almost always takes a complement.
我感到很疲惫。
Wǒ gǎn dào hěn píbèi.
I feel very exhausted.
他感到一阵寒意。
Tā gǎn dào yī zhèn hányì.
He felt a sudden chill.
语法 yǔfǎ · Grammar感到 + adj / noun phrase: 感到骄傲 proud · 感到难受 unwell · 感到惊讶 surprised. Can precede a clause: 感到一切都变了 "feel that everything has changed."
感觉gǎnjuéfeeling; impression; to feel (V & N)
V/N 动名词
感 gǎn + 觉 jué (to awaken; become aware — originally: waking from sleep, the moment of sudden consciousness). Felt awareness. Functions as both verb and noun — the most flexible word in the family. Includes intuition and subjective impression.
这种感觉很奇妙。
Zhè zhǒng gǎnjué hěn qímiào.
This feeling is wonderful. [noun]
我感觉他不太高兴。
Wǒ gǎnjué tā bù tài gāoxìng.
I get the sense he's not very happy. [verb]
辨析 biànxī · 感觉 vs. 感到 vs. 感受感觉 = subjective impression, includes gut feeling. 感到 = registering a more objective state. 感受 gǎnshòu (feel + 受 receive/undergo) = deeper, embodied, reflective experience. 感知 gǎnzhī (feel + 知 know) = perceptual cognition, academic register.
感受gǎnshòulived experience; to experience deeply
V/N 动名词
感 gǎn + 受 shòu (to receive; to undergo; to be a recipient). Something comes to you and is taken in. Emphasizes the experiential process, not just the end-state — richer than 感觉.
你有什么感受？
Nǐ yǒu shénme gǎnshòu?
What is your experience? What do you feel?
我想感受大自然的美丽。
Wǒ xiǎng gǎnshòu dàzìrán de měilì.
I want to experience the beauty of nature.
感恩gǎn'ēn Gratitude & Thanks — a spectrum
感谢gǎnxièto thank sincerely; gratitude (formal)
V/N 动名词
感 gǎn + 谢 xiè (to thank; originally: to pour out one's feelings, like water overflowing). Warmer and more sincere than 谢谢. Standard in writing, speeches, formal situations. Spectrum: 谢谢 (casual) → 感谢 (sincere/formal) → 感激 (deep, indebted) → 感恩 (philosophical virtue).
感谢您的帮助！
Gǎnxiè nín de bāngzhù!
Thank you sincerely for your help!
语法 yǔfǎ · Grammar感谢 + person + verb: 感谢你来 "thank you for coming." As noun: 表示感谢 biǎoshì gǎnxiè "to express gratitude."
感激gǎnjīdeeply grateful; moved by another's kindness
V 动词 dòngcí
感 gǎn + 激 jī (to stir; to agitate; rapids — originally: turbulent water). The heart stirred up by a significant kindness. Implies a debt that moves you emotionally. Stronger than 感谢; use for meaningful acts of help.
我非常感激你在我最难的时候帮助我。
Wǒ fēicháng gǎnjī nǐ zài wǒ zuì nán de shíhòu bāngzhù wǒ.
I'm deeply grateful you helped me in my hardest time.
成语 chéngyǔ · Idiom感激涕零 gǎnjī tì líng — "gratitude so deep that tears fall." 涕零 = tears streaming. Classical idiom for overwhelming thankfulness.
感恩gǎn'ēnto be grateful; honoring all grace received
V 动词 dòngcí
感 gǎn + 恩 ēn (grace; benevolence — the kindness of parents, teachers, heaven). The virtue of recognizing and honoring all goodness received. A core Confucian concept. The deepest gratitude word.
我们应该感恩父母的养育之恩。
Wǒmen yīnggāi gǎn'ēn fùmǔ de yǎngyù zhī ēn.
We should be grateful for the grace of our parents' upbringing.
文化 wénhuà · Culture感恩节 Gǎn'ēnjié = Thanksgiving. 恩 ēn appears in: 恩人 ēnrén benefactor · 恩情 ēnqíng kindness-bond · 忘恩负义 wàng'ēn fùyì ungrateful (lit. forget-grace-betray-duty).
感动gǎndòng Being Moved, Touched & Stirred
感动gǎndòngto be moved; to touch someone's heart
V 动词 dòngcí
感 gǎn + 动 dòng (to move). The heart set in motion by something beautiful, kind, or touching. One of the most important emotional verbs in Chinese.
这部电影让我非常感动。
Zhè bù diànyǐng ràng wǒ fēicháng gǎndòng.
This movie moved me deeply.
他的故事感动了所有人。
Tā de gùshì gǎndòng le suǒyǒu rén.
His story moved everyone.
语法 yǔfǎ · GrammarIntransitive: 我感动了. Transitive: 他感动了我. Causative 让/使 pattern very common. Related adjective: 感人 gǎnrén (touching, moving).
感慨gǎnkǎibittersweet swell of emotion; to sigh with feeling
V/N 动名词
感 gǎn + 慨 kǎi (to sigh; to lament — a person exhaling in grief). The bittersweet wave felt when contemplating time, change, and life's transience. Not quite sadness, not quite joy. Quintessentially literary.
回到故乡，他不禁感慨万千。
Huí dào gùxiāng, tā bùjīn gǎnkǎi wànqiān.
Returning to his hometown, he couldn't help but feel a thousand emotions.
感叹gǎntànto exclaim with feeling; to marvel
V 动词 dòngcí
感 gǎn + 叹 tàn (to sigh; to exclaim). When a feeling erupts as an exclamation. Note: 感叹号 gǎntàn hào = the exclamation mark (!).
他不禁感叹时光飞逝。
Tā bùjīn gǎntàn shíguāng fēishì.
He couldn't help but marvel at how quickly time flies.
感伤gǎnshāngmelancholy; touched with tender sadness
Adj 形容词 xíngróngcí
感 gǎn + 伤 shāng (wound; grief). The heart wounded by what it feels — a tender, melancholic state. The feeling of autumn, of parting, of beauty that cannot last.
秋天总让她感到感伤。
Qiūtiān zǒng ràng tā gǎndào gǎnshāng.
Autumn always makes her feel sentimental.
感召gǎnzhào Inspiration, Influence & Resonance
感染gǎnrǎnto infect; to inspire; to spread into
V 动词 dòngcí
感 gǎn + 染 rǎn (to dye; to stain; to spread — originally dye spreading through cloth). The same word for pathogen transmission and emotional contagion. Both are resonance spreading from one body to another — rooted in the cosmology of 气 qì.
她的热情感染了整个团队。
Tā de rèqíng gǎnrǎn le zhěnggè tuánduì.
Her enthusiasm inspired (lit. infected) the whole team.
他感染了流感。
Tā gǎnrǎn le liúgǎn.
He was infected with the flu.
感化gǎnhuàto reform through moral influence
V 动词 dòngcí
感 gǎn + 化 huà (to transform; to convert). To touch someone's heart so deeply they are morally transformed. A key Confucian concept: the ideal ruler governs not by force but by exemplary virtue that 感化s those around him.
老师用爱心感化了那个问题学生。
Lǎoshī yòng àixīn gǎnhuà le nàge wèntí xuésheng.
The teacher reformed the problem student through love.
感应gǎnyìngresonance; sympathetic response; induction
V/N 动名词
感 gǎn + 应 yìng (to respond; to echo). Used in physics (电磁感应 electromagnetic induction) and classical cosmology — the doctrine that Heaven and humanity are in constant resonant dialogue: 天人感应 tiān rén gǎnyìng.
天人感应是中国古代哲学的核心概念。
Tiān rén gǎnyìng shì Zhōngguó gǔdài zhéxué de héxīn gàiniàn.
"Heaven-human resonance" is a core concept of ancient Chinese philosophy.
X + 感X + gǎn "The Felt Sense of X" — Abstract Noun Pattern
构词洞见 gòucí dòngjiàn · Pattern Insight

Attaching 感 gǎn to the end of any noun or adjective creates an abstract noun meaning "the subjective, felt sense of X." Once internalized, you can decode and generate dozens of sophisticated psychological terms instantly. Japanese uses the identical pattern: 安全感 = 安全感 (anzenkan), 使命感 = 使命感 (shimmeikan).

其他qítā Other Key 感-Compounds
感情gǎnqíngemotion; affection; the bond of a relationship
N 名词 míngcí
感 gǎn + 情 qíng (emotion; sentiment; romantic feeling). The substance of a relationship — accumulated warmth and emotional history between people. Bigger than a single feeling.
他们之间有深厚的感情。
Tāmen zhījiān yǒu shēnhòu de gǎnqíng.
There is a deep bond between them.
辨析 biànxī · 感情 vs. 情感 qínggǎn感情 = colloquial, relational (feeling between people). 情感 qínggǎn = literary, abstract (art criticism, psychology). Warning: 感情用事 gǎnqíng yòng shì = "to act on emotion rather than reason" (pejorative).
感冒gǎnmàocold; flu; to catch a cold
V/N 动名词
感 gǎn + 冒 mào (to emit; to brave rashly). In traditional Chinese medicine, illness = pathogenic 气 qì entering the body. 感冒 = receiving and being overcome by wind-cold.
我感冒了，头很疼。
Wǒ gǎnmào le, tóu hěn téng.
I've caught a cold; my head really hurts.
俚语 lǐyǔ · Colloquial Slang对…不感冒 duì … bù gǎnmào = "not into / not interested in [something]." 我对这种音乐不感冒 = "I'm not into this kind of music." A vivid metaphorical extension worth memorizing.
感悟gǎnwùfelt insight; wisdom from experience
V/N 动名词
感 gǎn + 悟 wù (to awaken; enlightenment — the word for Buddhist satori). Insight that comes not from reasoning alone but from experience touching the heart.
旅行让我有了很多人生感悟。
Lǚxíng ràng wǒ yǒu le hěn duō rénshēng gǎnwù.
Travel gave me many life insights.
敏感mǐngǎnsensitive; a sensitive (topic / person / skin)
Adj 形容词 xíngróngcí
敏 mǐn (quick; alert; agile) + 感 gǎn (feel). Quick to feel — highly responsive. Used across registers: personality, skin, and political topics.
她是个很敏感的人，容易受伤。
Tā shì gè hěn mǐngǎn de rén, róngyì shòushāng.
She's a very sensitive person and is easily hurt.
这是个敏感话题。
Zhè shì gè mǐngǎn huàtí.
This is a sensitive topic.
成语chéngyǔ Idioms & Set Phrases
感同身受
gǎn tóng shēn shòu
to feel as if experiencing it oneself — deep empathy; sharing another's experience completely
Lit: feel-same-body-receive. The standard Chinese idiom for empathy. 我对你的遭遇感同身受。
感激涕零
gǎnjī tì líng
tears of gratitude streaming — overwhelmed by another's kindness to the point of tears
Lit: grateful-tears-fall. Highest register of thankfulness. 涕零 = tears streaming down the face.
感慨万千
gǎnkǎi wànqiān
ten-thousand emotions surging — overwhelmed by bittersweet feeling at life's passage
Go-to phrase for homecomings, reunions, witnessing great change. Appears constantly in memoirs and essays.
百感交集
bǎi gǎn jiāo jí
a hundred feelings converging — a complex rush of mixed emotions all at once
Lit: hundred-feelings-intersect-gather. For when emotions are too tangled and complex to name individually.

记忆法 jìyìfǎ · Master Retention Image

Imagine 感 gǎn as a tuning fork pressed against the heart. When something real strikes it — a kindness, a piece of music, a pathogen, a homecoming — the fork vibrates and the heart resonates. The upper element 咸 (universal, reaching all) tells you the vibration can travel anywhere: upward into gratitude 感恩, outward into others 感染, inward into insight 感悟, dissolving into bittersweet sighs 感慨. Every 感-word is a different shape that resonance takes. In Japanese: 感じる kanjiru — the same tuning fork, the same heart.
